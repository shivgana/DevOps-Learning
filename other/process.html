Which of the following is step 1 in the 7 Step Improvement Process? Prepare for action Define what you should measure Where are we now? Identify gaps in Service Level Agreement (SLA) achievement

  • Define what you should measure

Explanation:

The 7-Step Improvement Process is part of ITIL’s Continual Service Improvement (CSI) framework. The steps are:

  1. Define what you should measure
  2. Define what you can measure
  3. Gather the data
  4. Process the data
  5. Analyze the information and data
  6. Present and use the information
  7. Implement improvement

So, Step 1 is to “Define what you should measure” — determining the metrics and KPIs that align with business objectives and service goals.

  • Services that are under development

One organisation provides and manages an entire business process or function for another organisation. This is know as ___

  • Business Process Outsourcing

Explanation:

Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) is when one organization contracts out an entire business process or function (such as HR, payroll, customer support, finance, etc.) to another organization that specializes in managing and delivering that process.
Here’s how it compares to the other options:

  • Business Process Management (BPM) – A discipline focused on optimizing and managing internal business processes, not outsourcing.
  • Business Function Outsourcing – Not a standard term in ITIL or business practice.
  • Knowledge Process Outsourcing (KPO) – A specialized form of BPO that involves outsourcing high-level knowledge work like data analysis, legal services, or R&D.

So the most accurate and widely recognized term for outsourcing an entire business function is Business Process Outsourcing.

What are the types of activity within Demand Management?

  • Activity based, Business activity patterns and user profiles

Explanation:

Within Demand Management, the activities typically involve:

  • Activity-based analysis: Observing and measuring actual service usage.
  • Business Activity Patterns (BAPs): Understanding how business processes generate demand at different times.
  • User Profiles: Grouping users by their behavior and needs to better predict and manage demand.

These help in forecasting and shaping demand to optimize capacity and resources.

Why the others are less accurate:

  • Access Management is a separate process, not a type of Demand Management activity.
  • Analytical based is not the standard terminology used in ITIL for Demand Management activities.
  • Shaping user behavior is an objective of Demand Management but not classified as a type of activity alongside BAPs and user profiles.

So, the best choice is Activity based, Business activity patterns and user profiles.

Therefore, the Service Desk performs activities 1, 2 and 3 only.

Which of the following BEST describes when a Known Error record should be raised? A Known Error should not be raised until a temporary resolution or workaround has been found OA Known Error should be raised at any time that it would be useful to do so As soon as it becomes obvious that a quick resolution of the Problem will not be found Immediately following the Problem resolution, so a permanent historical record of all actions is retained in case of a recurrence

  • A Known Error should not be raised until a temporary resolution or workaround has been found

Explanation:

  • A Known Error record is created only after the problem has been analyzed and a workaround or temporary resolution is identified.
  • This allows support teams to quickly resolve incidents related to the Known Error while a permanent fix is being developed.
  • Raising a Known Error too early, before a workaround is known, would be less useful.

So, the best practice is to raise a Known Error once a workaround is available.
